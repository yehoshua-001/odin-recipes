Filipino Cuisine Edition

Filipino cuisine originated from Philippines, is a vibrant and flavorful reflection of the country's rich cultural history, blending influences from Malay, Spanish, Chinese, and American culinary traditions. It is characterized by a bold interplay of savory, sour, and sweet flavors, often achieved through the use of vinegar, soy sauce, citrus, and fermented ingredients. Rice is the cornerstone of every Filipino meal, typically served alongside a variety of meat, seafood, and vegetable dishes. Filipino food is also deeply communal, with meals often shared family-style in a tradition known as kamayan, where food is eaten together with hands on banana leaves. It is a cuisine that carries the heart and soul of the Filipino people — warm, generous, and deeply rooted in tradition.
